2026 | Gene expression profiling of dendritic cell tolerance dysfunction in women with Systemic lupus erythematosus. Authors: Ana Laura Hernández-Ledesma, Evelia Lorena Coss-Navarrete, et al. | bioRxiv DOI: https://doi.org/10.64898/2025.12.21.695630.

  • Abstract: Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) involves profound immune tolerance defects. In this study, we profiled dendritic cells from women with SLE and healthy donors, revealing interferon‑stimulated gene dominance, impaired IL‑10 signaling, and altered lipid metabolism. These findings highlight converging mechanisms that compromise dendritic cell tolerogenic capacity and contribute to disease pathogenesis.
  • In this study, I contributed to transcriptomic data analysis, statistical reporting, and figure design, integrating biological interpretation of dendritic cell dysfunction in systemic lupus erythematosus.
  • Data set: Regulatory Network Analysis of SLE-Associated Regulon Activity using SCENIC pipeline. Zenodo. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.17419882
